üì∫ Understanding TV Ratings & Revenue

TRP (Television Rating Point): Measures how many people are watching a show. A TRP of 2.5 means 2.5% of TV households are tuned in.

CPRP (Cost Per Rating Point): How much advertisers pay to reach 1% of the TV audience. If your show has low TRP, advertisers need more ad spots to reach their target, so your "cost per point" becomes less attractive.

Why This Matters: When your show gets lower ratings than promised, advertisers demand "make-goods" (free additional ad spots) or 20-30% discounts. This directly reduces the channel's revenue.

üì± What is "Brand Linkage"?

Brand Linkage: This measures whether viewers can remember which brand the ad was for after watching it.

The 5-Second Test: If someone stops watching your ad at the 5-second mark (which most people do), can they tell you what brand it was advertising?

The Problem: Most ads show the brand logo or name only in the last 5 seconds of a 30-second commercial. Since 70% of viewers skip before then, they see the ad but have no idea which company it's for. You're spending crores on advertising with less than 40% brand recall.

Late Brand Reveal

Primary Risk: Your brand name or logo appears only in the last 5 seconds of the ad. By then, 70% of viewers have already moved on.

Impact: Less than 40% of viewers can remember which brand the ad was for. You're paying to advertise your product category, not your specific brand. Your competitors benefit from your spending.

Attribution Failure

Weak Opening Hook

Primary Risk: The first 3 seconds of your ad are boring or confusing‚Äîgeneric shots, slow music, or unclear messaging that doesn't trigger any emotional response.

Impact: More than 50% of viewers mentally "check out" in the first 3 seconds. By 15 seconds, 70% have skipped. Message retention drops below 30%, meaning people watched your ad but can't remember what it said.

Hook Types That Actually Work

Hook Type What It Means When to Use
Emotional Turn Start with one emotion (happiness/peace) and quickly flip to another (tension/surprise). Example: Happy family scene suddenly interrupted by shocking news. Family products, brand films, storytelling campaigns where you want to create empathy
Conflict/Stakes Immediately show a problem or challenge that needs solving. Example: "Running late for an important meeting? Here's the solution..." Performance advertising, problem-solution products, challenger brands trying to stand out
Spectacle/Scale Start with something visually stunning or impressive that makes people say "wow." Example: Breathtaking landscapes, massive scale, unique visuals. Aspirational products, travel/experience marketing, tech launches, luxury brands
Sonic Signature A unique sound or jingle that instantly identifies your brand (like Britannia's "ting ting ti-ding" or Airtel's tune). Building long-term brand memory, audio-first platforms (radio/podcasts), mass market products
Character Quirk Start with an unusual or funny character behavior that stands out. Example: Amul's butter girl, Vodafone's ZooZoos. Youth brands, celebrity endorsements, creating shareable/meme-able content
